# Curriculum Mapping for Different Course Lengths

## Overview

This document provides educators with detailed curriculum mappings for implementing the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook across different course lengths. The mappings are designed to be flexible and adaptable to various educational contexts while maintaining the integrity of the core learning objectives.

## Course Duration Options

### 1-Week Intensive Course
**Target Audience**: Professional development, workshop format
**Total Hours**: 40 hours
**Daily Schedule**: 8 hours per day

#### Daily Breakdown:
- **Day 1**: ROS 2 Fundamentals (Chapters 1-2)
- **Day 2**: Robot Modeling (Chapter 3) + Simulation Basics (Chapter 4)
- **Day 3**: Advanced Simulation (Chapter 5) + Sensor Systems (Chapter 6)
- **Day 4**: AI Integration (Chapters 7-8)
- **Day 5**: Navigation (Chapter 9) + VLA Systems (Chapter 10)

### 2-Week Intensive Course
**Target Audience**: Summer programs, intensive training
**Total Hours**: 80 hours
**Weekly Schedule**: 40 hours per week

#### Weekly Breakdown:
- **Week 1**:
  - Day 1: ROS 2 Fundamentals (Chapter 1)
  - Day 2: Nodes, Topics, Services (Chapter 2)
  - Day 3: URDF for Humanoid Robots (Chapter 3)
  - Day 4: Gazebo Simulation (Chapter 4)
  - Day 5: Review and Lab Work

- **Week 2**:
  - Day 1: Unity Integration (Chapter 5)
  - Day 2: Sensor Simulation (Chapter 6)
  - Day 3: Isaac Sim (Chapter 7) + Isaac ROS (Chapter 8)
  - Day 4: Navigation (Chapter 9)
  - Day 5: VLA Systems (Chapter 10) + Integration

### 4-Week Standard Course
**Target Audience**: College semester course, professional certification
**Total Hours**: 60 hours
**Weekly Schedule**: 15 hours per week

#### Weekly Breakdown:
- **Week 1**: Module 1 - The Robotic Nervous System

  - Chapter 3: URDF for Humanoid Robots (3 hours)
  - Lab Session: ROS 2 Basics (4 hours)
  - Review/Q&A: (2.5 hours)

- **Week 2**: Module 2 - The Digital Twin (Simulation)
  - Chapter 4: Gazebo Simulation Fundamentals (3 hours)
  - Chapter 5: Unity Integration (4 hours)
  - Lab Session: Simulation Environment Creation (5 hours)
  - Review/Q&A: (3 hours)

- **Week 3**: Module 2 (continued) + Module 3 (beginning)
  - Chapter 6: Sensor Simulation and Integration (3 hours)
  - Chapter 7: Isaac Sim for Physical AI (4 hours)
  - Lab Session: Sensor Integration (5 hours)
  - Review/Q&A: (3 hours)

- **Week 4**: Module 3 (continued) + Module 4 (introduction)
  - Chapter 8: Isaac ROS for GPU-Accelerated Perception (4 hours)
  - Chapter 9: Navigation (Nav2) for Humanoid Robots (4 hours)
  - Chapter 10: VLA Systems (introduction) (2 hours)

## Flexible Module Implementation

### Module 1 Only (3 weeks)
- Week 1: Chapter 1 and 2 introduction
- Week 2: Chapter 2 and 3
- Week 3: Chapter 3 completion and review

### Module 2 Only (4 weeks)
- Week 1: Chapter 4 introduction
- Week 2: Chapter 4 completion and Chapter 5 start
- Week 3: Chapter 5 completion and Chapter 6 start
- Week 4: Chapter 6 completion and review

### Module 3 Only (5 weeks)
- Week 1: Chapter 7
- Week 2: Chapter 8
- Week 3: Chapter 9 (part 1)
- Week 4: Chapter 9 (part 2)
- Week 5: Integration and review

### Module 4 Only (2 weeks)
- Week 1: Chapter 10 introduction and concepts
- Week 2: Chapter 10 completion and integration

## Prerequisites by Course Length

### 1-2 Week Courses
- Basic programming knowledge (Python preferred)
- Familiarity with Linux command line
- Basic understanding of robotics concepts

### 4-8 Week Courses
- Same as above, plus:
- Some ROS 2 experience (helpful but not required)
- Basic understanding of simulation concepts

### 8+ Week Courses
- Same as above, plus:
- Some experience with AI/ML concepts (helpful)
- More advanced programming skills

## Assessment Timeline by Course Length

### 1-2 Week Courses
- Pre-course survey
- Daily check-ins
- Final practical assessment

### 4-8 Week Courses
- Weekly quizzes
- Mid-course project
- Final comprehensive assessment

### 8+ Week Courses
- Weekly assignments
- Midterm examination
- Project proposals and presentations
- Final examination

## Resource Allocation by Course Length

### Time Allocation Recommendations
- **Theory**: 30-40% of total time
- **Hands-on Practice**: 40-50% of total time
- **Review and Assessment**: 10-20% of total time

### Lab Session Recommendations
- **1-2 Week**: 4-6 hours per day
- **4-8 Week**: 3-4 hours per week
- **8+ Week**: 4-5 hours per week

## Adaptation Guidelines

### For Different Student Backgrounds
- **Beginner Students**: Extend time on foundational chapters (1-3)
- **Intermediate Students**: Standard pacing appropriate
- **Advanced Students**: Accelerate foundational chapters, extend advanced topics

### For Different Educational Contexts
- **Professional Development**: Focus on practical applications
- **Academic Courses**: Include theoretical foundations and research components
- **Self-Paced Learning**: Provide additional support materials and check-ins

## Technology Requirements by Course Length

### Minimum Requirements (1-2 Week)
- ROS 2 installation
- Basic simulation environment

### Standard Requirements (4-8 Week)
- Full ROS 2 setup
- Gazebo simulation
- Basic development tools

### Advanced Requirements (8+ Week)
- Full technology stack
- GPU-accelerated components
- Access to advanced simulation tools

This curriculum mapping provides flexibility for educators to adapt the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics content to their specific institutional requirements, student backgrounds, and time constraints while maintaining the educational integrity of the complete learning experience.
